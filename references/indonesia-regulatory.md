# Indonesia Mining — Regulatory & Political Risk Reference

Use when a project, acquisition, or due-diligence task involves Indonesian mineral assets. Regulatory settings here change fast (three major instruments landed in 2025 alone) — **verify current status by web search whenever an economic conclusion depends on a rule below, and date-stamp what you find.** Content below reflects the position as of mid-2026.

## 1. Overall direction

Since the 2020 Mining Law (UU 3/2020), policy has moved one way: resource nationalism plus fiscal extraction — centralized licensing, mass revocation of idle IUPs (2022), downstream-processing obligations, rising royalties, export levies, and capital controls. Pre-production assets with incomplete permits carry the maximum exposure to this trend.

## 2. Licensing reality check

- **IUP / IUP-OP**: check expiry vs mine-plan life; extensions are discretionary, not automatic.
- **RKAB** (annual work-and-budget approval): production legally requires it; approval timing is a known bottleneck and political lever.
- **IPPKH** (forest-area use permit): exploration and production permits are separate; a lapsed exploration IPPKH with no production IPPKH filed means realistic first-production is **2–3 years out**, whatever the seller's schedule says.
- **AMDAL** (environmental approval) and tailings (TSF) approvals: for restart cases the TSF permit is frequently the critical-path item.
- Model implication: treat seller production schedules as an optimistic branch; a permits-realistic delay scenario belongs in the base sensitivity set.

## 3. Foreign-ownership divestment (外资减持)

Foreign holders must divest to **49%** on a statutory schedule. **IDX listing does not exempt** — the test is the nationality of ultimate shareholders (PT Vale Indonesia still had to sell 14% to MIND ID in 2024 despite being listed). Structures totalling >49% effective foreign control have a legally mandated decay path; price control accordingly and pre-wire local-partner / staged-sale mechanics in transaction documents.

## 4. Fiscal terms (2025 reset)

- **Royalties — PP 18 & 19/2025** (effective April 2025): commodity-price-linked progressive rates. Gold ~3.75–5% (HMA-linked); coal progressive by benchmark price and license type.
- **Gold bullion/doré export tax** (from December 2025): ~10–15% when the reference price is above ≈US$3,200/oz. Combined government take on exported doré can reach ~16–20%; **domestic refining sale can cut this to roughly the royalty alone** — sales-routing is a first-order valuation variable, not an operating detail.
- **Coal export duty**: ~5% proposed for 2027; treat as a live scenario.
- **CIT 22%**; loss carry-forward 5 years.

## 5. Export-proceeds retention — PP 8/2025 (DHE SDA)

Non-oil-and-gas resource exporters must retain **100% of export proceeds onshore for at least 12 months** (fully applying to export declarations from 2026). Consequences for deal models:

- Dividends, shareholder-loan repayments and exit proceeds are systematically delayed; build the delay into IRR (typically −2 to −3 points of expected IRR per year of slip).
- Retained funds may be used for onshore operating costs; structure intercompany flows to spend trapped cash domestically.
- Banks offer DHE-collateralized facilities — a partial mitigation, at a cost.

## 6. Mining Law 4th Amendment (UU 2/2025)

Priority license allocation to religious organizations, cooperatives and SMEs alongside auctions; extended CCoW terms; overall a more discretionary, politically mediated licensing environment. Existing-permit renewals, expansions and RKABs are all exposed to it.

## 7. Community and artisanal mining

Gold districts (notably West Java) carry long histories of artisanal/illegal mining with mercury/cyanide legacies. Budget for community programs (PPM), security, and grievance mechanisms as recurring OPEX; treat community conflict as a stoppage risk in the execution factor, not an ESG footnote.

## 8. Political cycle and mitigations

- **2029 elections**: resource-populist measures tend to escalate pre-election; milestones scheduled into 2027–2029 sit inside that window.
- Mitigations: hold via a BIT jurisdiction with ICSID access (Singapore is common); political-risk insurance (MIGA or export-credit agencies — expropriation, transfer restriction, political violence; ~0.5–1.5%/yr of insured amount); price-adjustment and exit triggers for defined political events in the SPA.

## Quick screen (10 items)

IUP expiry vs LOM · RKAB status · IPPKH exploration *and* production · AMDAL/TSF · divestment path to 49% · royalty tier at current price · export tax vs domestic sale routing · DHE cash-trap in the cash-flow model · community/artisanal footprint · election-window exposure of key milestones.
